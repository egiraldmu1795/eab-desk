Construct 03 — Ag Condition Fade
Signal: USDA NASS Crop Progress & Condition (weekly state tables · nass.usda.gov) — a free, public government feed.
Signal source & raw input
Feed: USDA NASS Crop Progress & Condition (weekly). Key extracts (synthetic but shape-matched to real feed output for illustration):
  • Corn: 68% good-to-excellent, +1 pp week-over-week (national)
  • Soy: 65% good-to-excellent, flat to −1 pp
  • Key states (IL, IA, NE) showing slight improvement or stable high condition
Interpretation surface: better-than-feared crop condition can pressure prices (supply outlook improves). This is a short-horizon "report reaction" read, not a structural view on ag.
Committee deliberation (condensed excerpts)
Researcher
Public data shows corn condition ticking higher nationally; no major weather shock in the release. Historical pattern: condition beats can lead to 1–3 session fades in grain futures if no offsetting demand story. Context: current carry + export pace still supportive on the longer view.
Risk
Upside risk if later weeks reverse (drought / heat). Size must stay small; grain vol can spike on weather headlines. Correlation note: overlaps with energy (ethanol) and macro (USD). Conservative: cap at 1.5–2.5% portfolio risk budget even on a clean setup. Do not pyramid.
Bull
Strong crop = cheaper feed = margin expansion for livestock/ethanol downstream. If export sales data (separate) stays firm, price may not fade as much. A counter to "sell the news."
Bear
Classic "good news is bad news" for producers. Improved condition removes a bullish uncertainty premium; fast money often sells the beat. Precedent in prior seasons: 2–5% post-report moves lower on similar prints when no macro bid (illustrative, paper).
Portfolio Manager — decision
Decision (paper): short corn (ZC, or a relevant ETF proxy for the paper sim) on the condition beat. Small size. Thesis is mean-reversion to the report, not a macro call. Confidence: 6/10. Invalidation if the next 1–2 sessions hold above the report-day high or follow-on bullish news arrives. Rationale: "Condition beat removes the fear premium. Market will likely sell it until proven otherwise by demand or weather. Keep size tiny — this is a 1–3 day paper test of report reaction."
